The Basics

What is medical billing,
and why does it matter?

Before we get to money, here's the 2-minute version of what Halcyon actually does — because you'll need to explain this when someone asks.

Step 1
A Patient Sees the Doctor
The patient has insurance — Blue Cross, Aetna, Medicare, whatever. After the visit, somebody has to bill the insurance company to get the doctor paid. That process is called medical billing or Revenue Cycle Management (RCM). It's just: the doctor provided a service → insurance owes them money → someone has to claim it.
Step 2
The Billing Gets Complicated Fast
Insurance companies don't just hand over money. Claims get denied. Codes get rejected. Prior authorizations expire. Each payer has different rules. The money that's been earned but not yet collected is called Accounts Receivable (AR) — and poorly managed AR means the practice bleeds revenue every single month. The average practice loses 10–15% of earned revenue this way.
Step 3
Halcyon Handles It All
Halcyon manages the entire billing cycle for independent medical practices — from verifying insurance before the appointment all the way through chasing denied claims and collecting final payment. We operate through a HIPAA-compliant platform and a real-time client portal so practices see exactly where their money is at all times. Doctors get paid faster. Less denied. Less left on the table.
For partners: You do not need to understand the billing process in depth to generate leads. Your job is simply to connect us with the decision-maker at a medical practice. Halcyon's team handles the entire sales process, demo, contract, and onboarding. You just make the introduction.

Where to Look

Where to find leads — on every platform.

Independent medical practices are everywhere. Here's exactly how to find them, by channel. You don't need to be a salesperson — you just need to know where to look.

LinkedIn — Best Platform
The #1 Source for Decision-Makers
LinkedIn is 277% more effective than other platforms for reaching medical practice decision-makers. Physicians, practice managers, and office managers are all active here.
  • Search "practice manager" + your city and connect with 20–30/week
  • Search "family medicine" or "internal medicine" + "private practice"
  • Find doctors who list "MD, owner" or "physician owner" in their headline
  • Join groups like "Medical Practice Management" and "Healthcare Professionals Network"
  • Message: don't pitch immediately — ask about their billing situation first
  • 46% of physicians use LinkedIn for professional networking — they're there
Instagram & Facebook
Warm Leads Through Content
Instagram and Facebook are less direct but valuable for doctors who run "personal brand" practices or healthcare entrepreneurs.
  • Search hashtags: #privatepractice, #medicalpractice, #physicianowned
  • Find doctors who post about running their own practice (common in psychiatry, primary care, aesthetics)
  • Facebook Groups: "Private Practice Owners," "Physician Entrepreneurs," "Medical Practice Management"
  • DM doctors who post about billing problems, insurance headaches, or administrative stress
  • Be genuine — a personal message from someone they might know carries weight
Your Personal Network
The Highest-Converting Source
72% of medical billing contracts are won through referrals. If you know anyone in medicine — even loosely — this is your highest-value lead.
  • Family members or friends who are doctors, NPs, PAs, or therapists
  • Anyone whose family member works at a medical office or clinic
  • Doctors you've seen as patients at independent (non-hospital) practices
  • Dentists, chiropractors, physical therapists — they bill insurance too
  • Classmates from pre-med, nursing, or healthcare programs
  • Indian/South Asian professional network — large physician community
Google & NPI Registry
Find Practices in Any City
The NPI (National Provider Identifier) registry is a public government database of every licensed medical provider in the US. It's free and searchable.
  • npiregistry.cms.hhs.gov — search by specialty + city, get name + address
  • Google Maps: "[specialty] near me" → look for independent (non-hospital) practices
  • Call their front desk and ask for the "practice manager" or "billing department"
  • Check if their website looks old or says "billing questions" with a phone number — sign of in-house billing stress
  • Target specialties: Primary care, internal medicine, psychiatry, cardiology, orthopedics
Professional Events
Where Doctors Network
Medical professionals attend conferences, alumni events, and local networking groups. These are warm environments where an introduction feels natural.
  • Medical school alumni mixers and networking events
  • Local Indian Medical Association (IMA) or AAPI (American Assoc. of Physicians of Indian Origin) events
  • Chamber of Commerce healthcare committee events
  • Hospital volunteer programs where physicians congregate
  • Medical conferences in your city — healthcare is big in most metros
Target Specialties
Who Needs Us Most
Some specialties have higher billing complexity and more to gain from outsourcing. These are your highest-value leads.
  • Primary care / Internal medicine — high volume, often overwhelmed
  • Psychiatry / Mental health — complex insurance rules, high denial rates
  • Cardiology — expensive procedures, prior auth-heavy
  • Orthopedics / Surgery — complex coding, implant billing
  • Oncology — highest billing complexity, 6–8% market rate
  • Physical therapy — high visit volume, insurance parity issues
