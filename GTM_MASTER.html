Credentialing Strategy — Start Immediately on Signing

Payer credentialing (getting a provider enrolled with insurance payers) takes 30–90 days. You must start this the SAME DAY a client signs. If you wait even one week, you lose a week of paid claims later.

Credentialing action sequence after signing:

1. Collect: provider NPI, CAQH profile login, DEA number (if applicable), state medical license, malpractice certificate, CV, W-9.
2. Verify CAQH profile is complete and attested (re-attest if >120 days old — payers reject stale attestations).
3. Identify which payers the practice needs (ask for their payer mix from last 6 months of EOBs).
4. Submit credentialing applications to top 5 payers immediately. Track submission dates in a credentialing tracker tab in HubSpot or Google Sheets.
5. Follow up with each payer every 10 business days. Payers lose applications. Persistence is required.
6. While credentialing is processing: submit claims using the supervising or group NPI (if applicable) to avoid revenue gaps. Discuss this arrangement with the practice — it is standard and legal.

Section 17 — Failure Protocols

When Things Go Wrong.
The Exact Response for Each Scenario.

Every failure scenario has an optimal response. The companies that survive early stage are the ones with predetermined responses to adversity — not the ones that are best at improvising under pressure. Know these before you need them.

Trigger: Deal Goes Cold (No Response After 5 Touches)
Tag as "Cold" in HubSpot. Set a 90-day reminder task. Do NOT contact again before the reminder fires — you will only irritate them and damage any future chance. Move on immediately and redirect that energy to the next 10 prospects in your pipeline. The 90-day re-engagement email: "Hi [Name] — I reached out a few months ago about your billing setup. Circumstances change and timing matters. If anything has shifted for you — biller leaving, denial rate climbing, new provider starting — I'd love to reconnect. Still happy to do that complimentary audit if it's useful."
Trigger: Demo Goes Badly (Prospect Is Disengaged, Cuts It Short, or Ghosts)
Within 24 hours, send this exact email: "Hi [Name] — I want to be direct. I think I pitched you the wrong way yesterday. I focused on features when I should have focused on your specific situation. Can I take 5 minutes to correct that this week? I have a specific insight about [their specialty] billing in [their state] that I didn't get to." The re-pitch success rate is 20–30% for demos that went badly. The key is acknowledging the failure and offering a specific reason to try again — not just asking for another shot.
Trigger: Client Threatens to Leave or Is Clearly Unhappy
Naitik gets personally involved within 24 hours. Naitik's email: "Hi [Name] — I'm the CEO of Halcyon and I want to understand exactly what happened and fix it personally. This is not a support ticket. I'm asking to speak with you today. When works?" The save rate when the CEO shows up personally within 24 hours of a complaint is 60%+ across all service businesses. The CEO's personal attention signals that the client matters — not as a unit of revenue, but as a relationship. After the conversation: document every commitment made. Deliver on every one within the stated timeline.
Trigger: Outreach Volume Drops Below Targets for 3+ Days
Sunday night review. Every day below outreach target = approximately 6% fewer clients by year end at current conversion rates. The compounding math is brutal. The response is not guilt — it is a process audit: what specifically prevented hitting volume? Was it a time block that was interrupted? A tool that broke? A personal energy issue? Fix the specific cause. Do not accept "we'll do better next week" without identifying the root cause. Month 1–3: outreach volume is the only KPI that matters because everything — demos, proposals, clients — is downstream of it.
Trigger: First Client Has High Denial Rate After Onboarding
Do not hide it or wait for them to notice. Call the practice manager proactively: "I want to flag something I'm seeing in your first month of claims. Your denial rate is [X]% — higher than our target. I've already identified the root cause: [specific issue]. Here is our exact plan to bring it under [5%] in the next 30 days: [action steps]. I wanted you to hear this from me before your next billing statement." Proactive transparency on problems saves 80% of at-risk accounts. The clients who churn are usually the ones where the billing company tried to hide problems until they became unfixable.

Priority order for cost cuts: NOTHING in the outreach infrastructure (Lemlist, Sales Navigator, HubSpot) — these generate the revenue that covers all other costs.
